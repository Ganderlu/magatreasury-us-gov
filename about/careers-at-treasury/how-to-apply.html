How to Apply

Treasury job postings are commonly managed through USAJOBS. Each announcement lists duties, qualifications, required documents, and how applicants are evaluated. Following the announcement closely is the best way to submit a complete application.

Step-by-Step

  1. Find a job: search on treasury.usajobs.gov and filter by location, grade, and hiring path.
  2. Read eligibility: confirm you meet requirements (for example: public, internal, veterans, students).
  3. Build your resume: align your experience to the duties and specialized experience described in the posting.
  4. Prepare documents: upload required items before the deadline (transcripts, proof of eligibility, etc.).
  5. Submit and track: apply through the posting and use your application status to follow progress.

Resume Tips

  • Use the same keywords found in the job announcement.
  • Show results: what you delivered, the scale, and the outcome.
  • Be specific about tools, systems, and methods you used.
  • List dates, hours, and role level clearly when requested.

Common Reasons Applications Are Incomplete

  • Missing required documents (or wrong document type)
  • Resume does not show specialized experience clearly
  • Eligibility not documented (students, veterans, internal, etc.)
  • Application submitted after the closing date or cap
