Veterans Employment

Treasury values the skills and experience that veterans and military families bring to public service. Many roles align well with military experience—especially positions that require mission focus, teamwork, accountability, and disciplined execution.

How Military Skills Translate

  • Operations and logistics: planning, execution, and process reliability.
  • Leadership: team coordination, training, and performance management.
  • Security and risk: compliance discipline, threat awareness, and control practices.
  • Technical work: IT, cybersecurity, systems, and data operations.

Hiring Paths and Eligibility

Federal hiring can include different eligibility paths for veterans and service members. The exact options depend on the announcement and individual eligibility.

Helpful Steps

  • Look for job postings that list veteran eligibility in the “Hiring Path” section.
  • Prepare required documents early and upload them in your profile.
  • Translate military experience into results and responsibilities aligned to the job announcement.
  • Use keyword language from the announcement so your experience is easy to map to the role.

Resume Tips for Veterans

  • Describe mission outcomes, scale, and measurable results.
  • Explain acronyms and translate roles into civilian terms.
  • Highlight certifications, clearance history, and training where relevant.
  • Show leadership and accountability with clear examples.
