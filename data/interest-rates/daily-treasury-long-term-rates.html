Daily Treasury Long-Term Rates

Long-term Treasury rates are commonly used as a reference for longer horizon borrowing costs and for understanding market expectations about growth and inflation over time.

What Long-Term Rates Tell You

  • Borrowing conditions: long-term yields influence many fixed-rate loan products.
  • Expectations: reflect expectations about inflation and economic conditions.
  • Risk premiums: incorporate term and risk premiums for holding longer maturities.

Common Uses

  • Trend monitoring: track changes in long-run financing conditions.
  • Curve analysis: compare with short rates to evaluate steepness/flatness.
  • Planning: evaluate long-term interest rate exposure.
