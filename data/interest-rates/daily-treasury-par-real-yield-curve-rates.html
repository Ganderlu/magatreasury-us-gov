Daily Treasury Par Real Yield Curve Rates

“Real yield” measures yield after accounting for inflation. A real yield curve shows how inflation-adjusted yields vary across maturities and is used to compare real borrowing costs and real return expectations.

Key Ideas

  • Real vs nominal: nominal yields include inflation; real yields remove inflation effects.
  • Term structure: shows how real rates vary by maturity.
  • Interpretation: changes can reflect inflation expectations or real growth expectations.

Common Uses

  • Inflation analysis: compare nominal and real curves to infer inflation expectations.
  • Discounting: evaluate real discount rates for long-term projects.
  • Risk management: assess sensitivity to inflation and real-rate changes.
