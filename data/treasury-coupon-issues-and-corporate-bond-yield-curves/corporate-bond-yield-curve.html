Corporate Bond Yield Curve

A corporate bond yield curve shows the yields investors demand for corporate debt across maturities. It is used to understand corporate borrowing costs, credit conditions, and how corporate yields compare to Treasury yields.

Key Concepts

  • Credit spread: the difference between corporate yields and comparable Treasury yields.
  • Risk pricing: yields reflect default risk, liquidity risk, and market conditions.
  • Curve shape: changes can signal shifting risk appetite or growth expectations.

Common Uses

  • Financing conditions: track how easily companies can raise funds.
  • Stress monitoring: spreads often widen during periods of uncertainty.
  • Comparisons: compare across ratings, sectors, and maturities.
