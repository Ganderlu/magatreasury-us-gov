TIC Forms and Instructions

TIC forms and instructions define how certain information is collected and reported for TIC-related statistics. Instructions typically clarify definitions, reporting scope, timing, and how values should be recorded.

What Instructions Usually Include

  • Definitions: consistent terminology for security types and counterparties.
  • Scope: who must report and which transactions or positions are included.
  • Timing: reporting periods and due dates.
  • Validation: checks to reduce reporting errors and inconsistencies.

Why This Matters

  • Data quality: consistent reporting improves comparability across reporters.
  • Interpretation: understanding definitions prevents incorrect analysis.
  • Revisions: clear instructions reduce later corrections.
