Treasury Open Data

Treasury open data refers to public datasets, documentation, and tools that make Treasury information easier to find, use, and reuse. Open data efforts emphasize transparency, consistent definitions, and accessibility.

What You Typically Find in an Open Data Hub

  • Datasets: downloadable data and APIs where available.
  • Metadata: definitions, field descriptions, and update frequency.
  • Quality notes: revisions, limitations, and how to interpret values.

How People Use Treasury Data

  • Public understanding: explain budgets, debt, and cash flows.
  • Research: evaluate policy changes and economic conditions.
  • Applications: build dashboards and visualizations.
