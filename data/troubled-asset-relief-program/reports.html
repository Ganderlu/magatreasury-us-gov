TARP Reports

Reports summarize program activity, balances, repayments, and outcomes. They are commonly used to track progress over time and to provide public accountability for program operations.

What Reports Often Include

  • Totals: cumulative commitments, disbursements, and repayments.
  • Breakdowns: by program area or category.
  • Status: current outstanding amounts and program wind-down information.

How to Use the Information

  • Compare periods: track changes between reports.
  • Follow definitions: confirm what is included in each total.
  • Check revisions: some series can be updated or corrected over time.
