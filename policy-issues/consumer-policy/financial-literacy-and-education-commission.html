Financial Literacy and Education Commission

Financial literacy efforts focus on helping consumers understand budgeting, saving, credit, and long-term planning. This page provides a plain-language overview of how national financial education coordination is typically described and what topics are often covered in public materials.

Common Education Themes

  • Budgeting: tracking income and expenses and planning ahead.
  • Saving: emergency funds and goal-based saving.
  • Credit: credit scores, interest, borrowing costs, and repayment.
  • Fraud awareness: how to spot scams and protect personal information.

How Education Materials Are Used

  • Households: make better day-to-day and long-term financial decisions.
  • Educators: use consistent concepts and ready-made lesson plans.
  • Partners: tailor content to specific communities and life events.
