American Families and Workers

COVID-19 relief for families and workers generally focuses on helping households meet immediate needs, supporting employment stability, and reducing hardship during disruptions. The items below summarize the common types of assistance people look for and how relief programs are typically communicated.

Common Types of Support

  • Direct assistance: payments or benefits intended to help families cover essential expenses.
  • Unemployment-related support: temporary benefits that help workers manage income loss.
  • Housing and utility stability: support themes aimed at reducing displacement and keeping essential services available.
  • Tax-related relief: adjustments or credits that may increase after-tax support, depending on program design.

How to Determine Eligibility

Eligibility requirements can vary widely by program and time period. Applicants typically need to confirm residency or work status, income thresholds, program-specific criteria, and any documentation required by the administering agency.

What to Have Ready

  • Basic identity information and current contact details
  • Income or employment documentation, if required
  • Banking or payment delivery information, if applicable
  • Notices or letters associated with the specific program

Protecting Yourself From Scams

Relief programs can attract fraud attempts. Applicants should rely on official program channels and avoid sharing sensitive information with unverified contacts.

  • Be cautious of urgent messages demanding immediate payment.
  • Do not share one-time codes or passwords with anyone.
  • Confirm web addresses and phone numbers before responding.
  • If you encounter suspicious activity, use official reporting channels when available.
