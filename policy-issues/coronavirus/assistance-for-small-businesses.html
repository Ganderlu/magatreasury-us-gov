Small Businesses

Relief for small businesses generally focuses on keeping operations running, preserving jobs, and helping firms navigate temporary revenue shocks. Programs are often structured around lending, grants, tax provisions, and technical assistance.

Common Program Categories

  • Loan and loan guarantee programs: access to capital to cover payroll, rent, utilities, and operating expenses.
  • Grants and targeted relief: assistance for certain industries or impacted sectors, depending on authorizing guidance.
  • Tax relief and credits: provisions that may reduce tax burden or support retention and investment.
  • Payment flexibility: deferrals or modified repayment terms depending on program design.

What Businesses Typically Need to Apply

Application Readiness Checklist

  • Business identity documents and ownership information
  • Tax filings or financial statements for the relevant period
  • Payroll records and employee counts where applicable
  • Banking information for disbursement or repayment
  • Certifications and attestations required by the program

How to Evaluate a Relief Option

Relief programs often have eligibility conditions and allowable use rules. Before applying, businesses typically confirm the program’s purpose, usage limits, documentation, and any reporting obligations.

  • Confirm eligibility and deadlines early.
  • Understand allowable use of funds and recordkeeping requirements.
  • Plan for any follow-up reporting or compliance checks.
  • Keep copies of submissions and communications.
