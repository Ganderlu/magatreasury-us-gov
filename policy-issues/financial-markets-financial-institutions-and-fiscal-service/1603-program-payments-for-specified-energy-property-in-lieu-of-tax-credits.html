1603 Program

The “1603 Program” is commonly described as a payment program connected to specified energy property, where payments were made in lieu of certain tax credits. Discussions typically focus on eligibility, documentation, payment calculation, and compliance.

What Program Summaries Usually Include

  • Eligibility: what technologies or projects qualify and under what conditions.
  • Placed-in-service: timing and documentation that a project is operational.
  • Basis and calculation: how the payment amount is determined.

Administration and Controls

Common Compliance Topics

  • Documentation: invoices, certifications, and ownership records.
  • Reviews: checks for completeness and eligibility.
  • Audits: readiness for later verification and record retention.
  • Corrections: how adjustments or recaptures are handled when needed.

Why It Matters

  • Program integrity: controls protect funds and ensure consistent treatment.
  • Market impact: program design can influence investment timing and project economics.
  • Reporting: summaries help explain totals, timelines, and oversight outcomes.
