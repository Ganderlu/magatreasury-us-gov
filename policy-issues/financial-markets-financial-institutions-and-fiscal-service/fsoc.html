Financial Stability Oversight Council (FSOC)

FSOC is commonly described as a coordinating body that monitors risks to the financial system. Its work focuses on identifying vulnerabilities, improving information sharing, and supporting a stable and resilient financial system.

What “Financial Stability” Monitoring Looks Like

  • Vulnerabilities: leverage, liquidity mismatch, and interconnectedness.
  • Market functioning: indicators of stress, volatility, and liquidity conditions.
  • Emerging risks: new products, structures, or exposures that may grow quickly.

Common Themes in Stability Reports

Topics That Show Up Frequently

  • Nonbank finance: risks outside traditional banking channels.
  • Operational resilience: technology, cybersecurity, and continuity planning.
  • Data gaps: the need for comparable metrics across markets and firms.
  • Coordination: aligning responses across agencies and stakeholders.

Why It Matters

  • Supports early identification of system-wide vulnerabilities.
  • Promotes consistent approaches across regulatory and policy bodies.
  • Improves transparency around major stability priorities.
