Policy Issues • Terrorism and Illicit Finance

311 Actions

“311 actions” is a common shorthand for special measures that can be applied to address money laundering concerns involving a foreign jurisdiction, financial institution, type of account, or class of transactions. These measures are used to reduce risk exposure and protect the financial system.

What These Actions Aim to Achieve

  • Reduce access: make it harder for illicit actors to use certain channels.
  • Increase transparency: require more information on ownership and flows.
  • Change incentives: shift behavior by raising compliance costs or limiting services.

Operational Impacts

What Institutions Usually Need to Do

  • Update controls: screening rules, monitoring, and escalation processes.
  • Adjust due diligence: identify beneficial owners and high-risk counterparties.
  • Recordkeeping: retain evidence of decisions and checks performed.

How to Read a Notice or Guidance

  • Scope: which parties, products, or geographies are covered.
  • Required actions: what processes must be changed.
  • Effective dates: when requirements begin and how transitions are handled.
