Terrorist Finance Tracking Program

Terrorist finance tracking refers to efforts that use financial information to identify and disrupt terrorist financing networks. Tracking is typically described as combining data analysis, investigative leads, and cooperation with partners to map networks and stop the flow of funds.

Why Financial Tracking Matters

  • Networks leave patterns: transfers, counterparties, and timing can reveal structure.
  • Disruption opportunities: identifying key nodes can reduce network effectiveness.
  • Evidence: financial trails can support investigations and prosecutions.

What Analysts Commonly Look For

Signals and Patterns

  • Counterparty networks: repeated relationships across accounts and entities.
  • Structuring behavior: breaking transactions into smaller parts to avoid thresholds.
  • Rapid movement: fast layering through multiple intermediaries.
  • Geographic risk: corridors with higher exposure to illicit finance risks.

How Programs Are Often Described

  • Data governance: clear rules for access, retention, and oversight.
  • Controls: procedures to protect sensitive information.
  • Coordination: sharing relevant insights with appropriate partners.
