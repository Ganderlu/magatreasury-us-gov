Policy Issues • Terrorism and Illicit Finance

Asset Forfeiture

Asset forfeiture is a legal process that can transfer ownership of property connected to certain crimes. It is commonly described as a way to disrupt criminal organizations by removing proceeds of crime and the tools used to commit offenses, while following due process requirements.

How Forfeiture Supports Illicit Finance Disruption

  • Removes incentives: reduces the ability to profit from illegal activity.
  • Disrupts networks: prevents reuse of assets that support operations.
  • Supports investigations: financial tracing clarifies how funds moved.

Common Steps (High Level)

Typical Flow

  • Tracing: identify assets and ownership structures.
  • Seizure: legal action to take custody of targeted property.
  • Adjudication: a process to determine whether forfeiture is warranted.
  • Disposition: management and final handling of forfeited assets.

Governance and Controls

  • Documentation: clear records of decisions and supporting evidence.
  • Oversight: policies and review mechanisms to ensure appropriate use.
  • Transparency: reporting and audit readiness for program integrity.
